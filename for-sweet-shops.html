Common questions about Sweet Shops POS

Real questions from sweet shops owners using HisaabNow.

How does weight-based pricing work (kg, gram, half-kg)?
Set a price per kg for each mithai (e.g., kaju katli ₹1,200/kg). At the counter, type the weight: 250g auto-calculates ₹300, 1.5kg auto-calculates ₹1,800. No mental math, no rounding errors. Critical for any sweet shop billing software serving Indian customers.
Does it remind me about festival stock (Diwali, Holi, Eid)?
Yes — Munim sends festival alerts 2 weeks before each major festival. Diwali: order ladoo dabbas, dry fruits, gift boxes. Holi: gujiya, thandai mix. Raksha Bandhan: rakhi-themed boxes. Eid: sheer khurma ingredients. Based on previous years' sales patterns. Indian-festival-aware unlike generic halwai billing apps.
How does expiry tracking work for fresh mithai?
Set shelf-life per item (kaju katli: 7 days, soan papdi: 30 days, fresh chamcham: 2 days). When you log a fresh batch, the app counts down. 2 days before expiry, it suggests "discount soan papdi 20% to clear stock." Catches expiring inventory before you have to throw it away.
Can I handle gift box orders with custom mithai mixes?
Yes. Build a gift box bill with multiple items (kaju katli 250g + soan papdi 250g + dry fruits 100g + box ₹50). Total weight, total price, customer name — all on one receipt. Common for Diwali corporate orders. Save the box recipe to reuse for repeat orders.
What about wholesale or bulk orders to other shops?
Mark a customer as "wholesale" — they get a different price tier (your wholesale rate, not retail MRP). Bulk orders, delivery notes, GST invoices for B2B sales — all handled. Profit reports separate retail counter sales from wholesale revenue so you know your margin mix.
Does it work for namkeen / savory counter alongside sweets?
Yes — most sweet shops also sell namkeen (sev, bhujia, mathri). Add them as separate categories with the same weight-based pricing. Reports show sweet vs namkeen revenue separately so you know which side of the counter actually earns. Many of our mithai shop POS users sell both.
