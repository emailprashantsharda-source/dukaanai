Common questions about Cafés POS

Real questions from cafés owners using HisaabNow.

How does recipe-based cost tracking work for coffee?
Define each menu item once with its recipe — for cappuccino: 18g beans, 150ml milk, 1 cup, 1 paper sleeve. Set ingredient costs (beans ₹2,400/kg, milk ₹62/L). The app calculates real cost per cappuccino (~₹47) automatically. Every sale shows actual profit, and ingredient stock auto-deducts. This is what makes it the best recipe costing app for Indian cafés.
Does voice billing work in a noisy cafe environment?
Voice billing uses your phone's microphone with browser speech recognition. In a typical cafe (background music + 8-10 customers chatting) it works well — say "do cappuccino, ek cheese sandwich" and items go to the bill. For very loud environments (espresso grinding right next to phone), tap-billing is faster. Most owners use voice during prep and tap during peak rush.
Can I split costs across different coffee bean batches?
Yes. Track ingredient batches separately — when you buy a new lot of beans at a different price, log it as a new batch. The app uses weighted-average cost so your profit numbers stay accurate even when bean prices fluctuate. Critical for cafés serving specialty single-origin coffees.
How do I handle combos, add-ons, and customizations?
Create combos as menu items (e.g., "Cappuccino + Croissant" priced at ₹240 instead of ₹280 separately). Add-ons like extra shot, oat milk, syrup are modifiers — tap an item, add the modifier, price adjusts automatically. Customizations like "less sugar" attach as notes to the kitchen ticket without changing price.
Can I print kitchen tickets to a separate printer?
Yes. Connect a Bluetooth thermal printer in the kitchen — order placed at counter prints on the kitchen printer instantly. Customer receipts print on the counter printer (or go to WhatsApp). Two separate printers, one phone, no shouting orders across the cafe.
What happens during the morning rush — is it fast enough?
Built for the 7am rush. Tap-billing averages 8 seconds per order from open to print. Voice billing is 3-5 seconds when it works. Pre-saved customer profiles speed up regulars ("Aditya's usual" = double espresso). At 60 orders/hour the app keeps up — that's beyond most cafés' actual peak.
